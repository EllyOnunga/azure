Family Recipes

James Ogutu's Journey from Mombasa to Capital Center

February 1, 2026 By James Ogutu Our Story

I still remember the smell of my grandmother's kitchen in Mombasa's Old Town. Every morning, she'd start the day by grinding spices with a traditional kinu (pestle), the rhythmic sound echoing through our coral-stone house. Those were my first cooking lessons.

Growing Up in the Swahili Coast

I was born in Mombasa's Old Town, where the air is always thick with the aroma of spices. My grandmother, Bi Mwanaisha, was known throughout the neighborhood for her wali wa samaki (fish rice) and vitumbua (coconut rice cakes).

At age 8, I was already helping her prepare the evening meal. By 12, I could make her famous peshkari (spiced fish) from memory. But it wasn't just about the cooking-it was about the stories.

"Every dish has a story. When you cook with love, those stories become part of the food." - Bi Mwanaisha

My Culinary Journey

1995 - First Kitchen Job

Started as a dishwasher at Mombasa's Serena Beach Hotel. Watched, learned, and absorbed everything I could.

2000 - Head Chef Position

Became head chef at a beach resort in Diani. Developed my signature style blending traditional Swahili with modern techniques.

2010 - Nairobi

Moved to Nairobi to explore the capital's food scene. Worked at top restaurants, learning new techniques while staying true to my roots.

2024 - Azure Bay

Opened Azure Bay at Capital Center. Finally, a place where I could serve the food of my childhood to everyone.

What Drives Me

Every dish I create at Azure Bay carries my grandmother's touch. The spices she taught me, the techniques she perfected over 70 years, and most importantly, the love she put into every meal.

When you dine at Azure Bay, you're not just eating seafood-you're tasting four generations of coastal cooking tradition. My grandmother's recipes, adapted for modern palates but never compromising on authenticity.

Signature Dishes

Some of my signature dishes that I learned from my grandmother:

  • Samaki wa Kupaka: Grilled fish in coconut and spice sauce
  • Prawns Piri Piri: Spicy grilled prawns with Swahili chilies
  • Coastal Lobster Thermidor: A French classic with Swahili influences
  • Traditional Fish Curry: My grandmother's recipe, unchanged

I invite you to taste these stories. Every bite is a memory, every flavor is a lesson learned in my grandmother's kitchen.
