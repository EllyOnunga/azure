The Art of Grilling

Traditional Kenyan Coastal Techniques

February 10, 2026 By Faith Wanjiku Recipes

There's something magical about seafood grilled over open flames. The smoke infuses the fish with a subtle smokiness, while the high heat creates that perfect caramelized exterior. At Azure Bay, we've perfected the art of grilling using techniques passed down through generations of Kenyan coastal families.

The Foundation: Choosing Your Fish

Great grilled seafood starts with selecting the right fish. In Kenya, we're blessed with an abundance of fresh options:

  • Tuna (Nduva): Firm flesh, mild flavor, perfect for high heat
  • Swordfish (Mshale):Meaty, steak-like texture
  • Red Snapper (Changu): Classic coastal choice
  • Prawns (Kamba): Sweet, quick to cook
  • Octopus (Pweza): Traditional favorite, requires预处理

Traditional Techniques

1. The Swahili Marinade

Our grandmother's recipe uses a blend of lemon juice, garlic, ginger, and Swahili spices. The acid tenderizes the fish while the spices create depth of flavor. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, but never overnight—the acid can "cook" the fish too much.

2. Coconut Charcoal

Traditional coastal grilling uses coconut charcoal, which burns hotter and imparts a subtle sweet smoke flavor. This is what gives our grilled dishes their distinctive taste.

3. The 3-Minute Rule

Flip your fish only once. Cook for 3 minutes per side for medium-rare. The fish should still have a slight translucency in the center—that's when it's most succulent.

4. Banana Leaf Wrapping

For more delicate fish, wrap in banana leaves before grilling. This steams the fish while adding a subtle herbal flavor and keeping it incredibly moist.

Pro Tips from Our Grill Master

  • Always bring fish to room temperature before grilling
  • Oil the grill grates, not the fish
  • Use tongs—piercing the fish releases juices
  • Let grilled fish rest for 2 minutes before serving

"Grilling is patience. You can't rush fire. The flames teach you to be calm, to wait for perfection." — Faith Wanjiku, Grill Master

Try It at Home

Want to recreate the Azure Bay experience at home? Here's our basic grilled fish recipe:

  • 1 lb fresh red snapper or tuna
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Mix all ingredients (except oil), marinate fish for 30 minutes. Brush with oil, grill over high heat for 3 minutes per side. Serve with kachumbari and rice.
