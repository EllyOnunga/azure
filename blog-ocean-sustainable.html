From the Indian Ocean

A Journey of Sustainable Seafood in Kenya

January 15, 2026 By James Ogutu Sustainability

Every morning, long before the sun rises over the Kenyan coast, our fishing partners in Malindi and Lamu cast their nets into the rich waters of the Indian Ocean. This isn't just fishing—it's a tradition that dates back centuries, connecting the Swahili coast with the rest of the world.

Our Commitment to Local Communities

At Azure Bay, we believe that great seafood starts with responsible sourcing. That's why we've built direct relationships with fishing communities along the Kenyan coast. When you dine with us, you're not just enjoying a meal—you're supporting over 200 families whose livelihoods depend on sustainable fishing practices.

Our partnership with the Kenya Marine and Fisheries Research Institute (KMFRI) ensures that every catch meets strict sustainability standards. We only source fish that are abundantly available, using methods that protect marine ecosystems.

"The ocean gave us everything. We must give back to the ocean." — Saidi Hassan, Fisherman from Lamu

From Catch to Kitchen

The journey from the Indian Ocean to your plate at Azure Bay is carefully orchestrated. Here's how we ensure freshness:

  • Early Morning Catch: Our fishermen depart at 4 AM to catch the freshest fish
  • Immediate Icing: Fish are placed on ice within minutes of being caught
  • Cold Chain Transport: Specially designed refrigerated vehicles maintain optimal temperatures
  • Quality Check: Every fish is inspected by our chefs upon arrival

Protecting Kenya's Marine Heritage

Kenya's coastline stretches over 500 kilometers, harboring some of the most diverse marine life in the Indian Ocean. From the coral reefs of Malindi to the mangrove forests of Gazi, our waters are treasure troves of biodiversity.

We're proud to support the Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) established by the Kenyan government. A portion of every Azure Bay meal goes toward marine conservation efforts, helping preserve these waters for future generations.

What This Means for You

When you choose Azure Bay, you're making a choice that benefits:

  • Local fishing communities and their families
  • Marine ecosystems and ocean health
  • Authentic Kenyan coastal cuisine
  • Sustainable tourism in Kenya

Our seafood isn't just fresh—it's a story of tradition, sustainability, and the incredible people who bring the ocean's bounty to your table. We invite you to taste the difference that responsible sourcing makes.

James Ogutu

Head Chef & Co-Founder
