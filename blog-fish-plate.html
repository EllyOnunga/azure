Mwala Beach to Nairobi

How Fresh Fish Reaches Your Plate

January 20, 2026 By Kevin Ochieng Supply Chain

Have you ever wondered how that delicious grilled tuna on your plate at Azure Bay made its way from the Indian Ocean to our kitchen? It's a fascinating journey that involves dedication, precision, and a deep respect for quality.

The Journey Begins: Mwala Beach

Mwala Beach, located along the Kilifi coast, is one of Kenya's most productive fishing grounds. Here, local fishermen have been plying their trade for generations, using traditional dhows and modern fishing vessels.

Our partnership with Mwala Beach fishermen ensures we get the first pick of the day's catch. Every evening, our procurement team coordinates with boat captains to understand what varieties will be available the next morning.

4:00 AM - The Catch

Fishermen cast their nets in the pre-dawn darkness.

6:00 AM - Sorting & Icing

Catch is sorted and immediately placed in insulated containers with ice.

8:00 AM - Departure

Refrigerated trucks depart for Nairobi, covering 450km.

2:00 PM - Arrival

Our chefs inspect every fish for your meal.

Our Cold Chain Promise

  • GPS-Monitored Trucks: Real-time tracking
  • Temperature Logging: Continuous monitoring below 4°C
  • Professional Handling: Fish stored within 15 minutes of arrival
  • Daily Sourcing: Never frozen—everything served fresh daily

"In Kenya, we say 'Moto moto'—fresh and hot. That's what we deliver every single day." — Mwala Beach Fishermen's Cooperative

Why This Matters

The difference between fresh and frozen fish is profound. When fish is properly chilled immediately after catching, it retains its natural juices, firm texture, and delicate flavor. This is what makes Azure Bay seafood truly exceptional.
